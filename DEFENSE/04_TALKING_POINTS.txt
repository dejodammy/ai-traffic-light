================================================================================
  PRESENTATION TALKING POINTS - a ~5 minute spoken flow
================================================================================

Use this as the spine of your talk. Speak to it; don't read it word for word.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  OPENING (30 sec) - the problem
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  "Lagos has some of the worst traffic congestion in the world. A big part of
   the problem is that traffic lights run on fixed timers - they give the same
   green time to every direction regardless of how many cars are actually
   waiting. So one road sits empty on green while another is jammed on red.
   My project asks: can an AI controller do better by adapting to real demand?"

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  THE IDEA (45 sec) - what I built
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  "I built an intelligent traffic-light controller using Deep Reinforcement
   Learning - specifically a Deep Q-Network. It looks at how congested each of
   the four approaches is, and every few seconds it decides which direction
   should get the green, in order to clear the junction as fast as possible.
   I trained and tested it on a real Ikeja junction inside SUMO, an open-source
   traffic simulator."
   [SHOW: architecture / rl_loop figure]

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  HOW IT WORKS (60 sec) - state / action / reward
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  "The agent sees a STATE of 12 numbers - queue length, waiting time and
   density for each approach. Its ACTION is choosing the next green phase. Its
   REWARD is the reduction in congestion, so it's literally rewarded for
   clearing queues, with a small penalty so it doesn't flicker the lights.
   I trained it in two stages: first it imitates a sensible expert rule to get
   a good starting point fast, then reinforcement learning fine-tunes it over
   200 episodes so it can plan ahead and beat that rule."

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  THE DEMO (90 sec) - SHOW, don't just tell
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  "Let me show it running."
   [RUN: scripts\1_Watch_Trained_AI_Live.bat]
  "Here's the trained AI on the real junction. Watch it give green to the
   busiest direction - the queue stays low."
   [RUN: scripts\2_Watch_FixedTime_Baseline.bat]
  "Now the same traffic under a normal fixed timer. The queues build up because
   the timer can't see them. Same cars, same junction - the only difference is
   the controller."
   (Fallback if live demo won't run: play media\DQN_on_real_Ikeja_junction.gif)

  OPTIONAL - the perception side (the "eyes"):
   [RUN: scripts\4_YOLO_Live_Webcam_Count.bat or 5_YOLO_Count_On_Sample_Video.bat]
  "And here's how it would see the real world: a YOLO detector boxes and counts
   the vehicles live - that count is exactly what feeds the controller's state
   in a real deployment, on a camera and a Raspberry Pi."

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  RESULTS (45 sec) - the numbers
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  "On the real Ikeja junction - the realistic 4-phase signal with variable
   traffic - the AI cut the average queue by about 14% and the average waiting
   time by about 16% versus the fixed timer, winning on both. The real value is
   responsiveness: it adapts in real time and skips empty approaches instead of
   waiting out a fixed cycle. And the pattern held across demand levels - it
   wins wherever traffic is uneven and variable. That tells me it learned a real
   strategy, not just memorised one situation."
   [SHOW: baseline_comparison / delay_reduction figure]

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  LIMITATIONS + FUTURE (30 sec) - be honest, it scores points
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  "It's a single-junction prototype trained on one demand pattern, so it doesn't
   transfer zero-shot to other junctions and it only wins when traffic is below
   saturation and uneven. The next steps are training across many junctions and
   demand patterns, and deploying it on a Raspberry Pi with a camera and physical
   lights - which the system is already designed for."

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  CLOSE (15 sec)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  "In short: an adaptive, learning controller clearly beats a fixed timer on
   uneven traffic - exactly the conditions Lagos faces every day. Thank you -
   I'm happy to take questions."
